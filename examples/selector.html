NOTE: Use the console for this example! Press CTRL + SHIFT + I to open the console, or press F12 to open the console.

Hey look, I am a paragraph

g.Selector("<p>")

My classname is 'epic-text'

g.Selector(".epic-text")

My ID is 'epic-id'

g.Selector("#epic-id")

The red box has a 'red-box' as classname, the blue box has 'blue-box' as classname.

Click the button to reveal the blue box inside the red box, in the console.

g.Selector(".blue-box in .red-box")

The child selector doesn't work for ID's yet, but here are the boxes anyway.

But it should look like this:

g.Selector("#blue-box in #red-box")

The red box has 'dupo_box' as element name, the blue box has 'dupo_child_box' as element name.

Click the button to reveal the blue box inside the red box, in the console.

g.Selector("<dupo_child_box> in <dupo_box>")

It works in many ways:

g.Selector(".myChild in .myParent") g.Selector(".myChild in <div>") g.Selector("<div> in .myParent") g.Selector("<div> in <header>")

Event listeners on elements.

g.Selector(".click-sayhi").On("click", myFunction1);

Style attributes on elements.

g.Selector(".new-box").Css("background", "green");

And there are other functions as well! Check out the documentary for more information.
